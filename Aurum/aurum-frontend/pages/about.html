Our Story

The Beginning

Born in Paris,
Raised on Passion

In 2012, Chef Jp Mbaga walked away from a prestigious position at a three-star restaurant in Rome with a single conviction: that the world deserved to experience Italian cuisine not as a tradition frozen in time, but as a living, evolving art form.

He opened the first Aurum on Via della Luce in Paris's Brera district — a narrow, sixty-seat dining room lit by candlelight and a single chandelier he'd found at a Florentine flea market. The name, Latin for gold, reflected both the colour of good olive oil and the standard to which every dish would be held.

Within eighteen months, the first Michelin star arrived. By 2016, there were three.

The Chef

JP Mbaga

Born in Bologna in 1971 to a family of farmers, Jp Mbaga grew up understanding that great food starts in the earth, not the kitchen. He trained under Gualtiero Marchesi in Milan before spending a decade in France, absorbing technique without ever surrendering his Italian soul.

His cooking philosophy is deliberate restraint: three ingredients on a plate, each at its absolute best. No distraction. No decoration. Just the honest flavour of something that was alive recently and handled with total respect.

He visits his suppliers in Tuscany and Sardinia every quarter. He still personally tastes every new dish before it reaches a single table.

Jp Mbaga

Provenance
